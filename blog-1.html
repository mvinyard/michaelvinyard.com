Running Blog - 2019 SF Marathon Prep Week # 1

Published April 17, 2019 - Michael Vinyard

I am currently training for the 2019 San Francisco Marathon. I completed my first marathon in September of 2018, the Maine Marathon in Portland, ME. I signed up for this marathon on the day of the Boston Marathon in 2018 - I was on the treadmill at the gym, watching the coverage of the marathon and I was just feeling inspired. At first, I just considered this a bucket list item for me - I knew I had to train and that I had to take it seriously to be able to complete the marathon without injury, but I did not expect to get so sucked into the culture. However, I can't say I blame myself - the people who do these races (and longer) are awesome and inspiring.

It started with podcasts about running - when I started I was overall disatisfied with my physical fitness; this was actually a major reason for me wanting to do a marathon in the first place. I wanted to get in shape and I needed something to hold me accountable. So, being frustrated with my fitness and slow running speed, I turned to podcasts to learn more. I also found them quite inspiring. This was my first step into the culture of distance running. I then started to get in touch with local running clubs - one of my favorites that I still try to make it to is Boston Road Runners. There are a ton of novice and experienced runners of all speeds that are graet to learn from and talk to during a Saturday morning on the Charles River.

As my training for SF 2019 ramps up, I will post more about my plan and keep this blog updated with how things are going! I have linked my Strava account at the bottom of the page and I'll try to push my HTML and CSS abilities to link the media from Strava in cool ways.

Week 1: I got off to a late start for this training cycle. I had a lot going on with school and life and this prevented me from maintaining the ~40 mi/week schedule I had been following back in January - not to mention the weather in Boston can be relatively disruptive to a good outdoor training schedule if you're not careful.

I got back into the swing of things on Tuesday evening. I typically don't like running at night, but it's what my schedule has allowed lately, so I am suffering through it. I did make it all the way through until Sunday - I am unsure if I want to go for a run-streak or take a schedule day off... I suppose I could always squeeze in a quick mile if I wanted to count that as my ~minimum~ to keep a run streak alive.

The long run. I did a psuedo-long run this week. I wouldn't really call this a long run; it was only 6 miles and the rest of my weekly mileage (31 miles in total) was made up of daily 5 mile runs, BUT it's a matter of laying the groundwork for the future weekend long runs to come. One thing I definitely let slip (and regretted it) in training for the marathon I did last year was not sticking to a regimented long run schedule. I was too lenient with myself and I paid the price for it come race day. I actually planned out the remainder of my long runs this weekened and they are as follows:

  • April 20: 6 mi (DONE)
  • April 27: 7 mi
  • May 4: 8 mi
  • May 11: 10 mi
  • May 18: 11
  • May 25: 12
  • June 1: 14 mi
  • June 8: 15 mi
  • June 15: 16 mi
  • June 22: 18 mi
  • June 29: 20 mi
  • July 6: 22 mi Cue the beginning of taper!
  • July 13: 12 mi
  • July 20: 10 mi
  • July 28: Race Day - 26.2 mi

In looking at this schedule, the one thing that comes to mind is that it is a LOT of long runs. I think I am much more prepared this time around, however to handle such a schedule. I think I got above 30 miles per week (mpw) maybe two or three times before my first marathon due to a tough schedule. This time, I am starting out at greater than 30 mpw. The week of the 22 miler, I hope to be up around 70 mpw, but we'll see how it goes. I recently heard a quote, paraphrased from Meb Keflezighi that it's better to be undertrained and healthy than overtrained and injured. With that many long runs scheduled, I would feel pretty comfortable accomodating one or two to stay in tip-top shape. I have struggled with plantar fasciitis for a while, so managing that is always a chore.
