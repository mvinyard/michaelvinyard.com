Life Update

I have moved to two new primary laboratories!

Updated July 1, 2019 - Michael Vinyard

I spent roughly two years in the Liau Lab working on uncovering the biological mechanism of LSD1 in AML. We used genome editing tools as well as other modern genomics, transcriptomics, and epigenomics interrogation methods to set up experiments that stratify the presence or absence of a mechanism-based chemical inhibit of LDS1. I was a member of Brianâ€™s first cohort of graduate students; the lab grew quickly in those two years and I had the opportunity of working with tons of great people. I owe them a ton.

While it was quite a difficult decision for me to shift my focus away from what I was investigating in the Liau Lab, I am very excited to announce that I have joined the labs of Professors Gad Getz and Luca Pinello. Gaddy and Luca are both affiliates of the Broad Institute, Massachusetts General Hospital, and Harvard Medical School.

While I have shifted my focus to a dry-lab, computational biology setting, I intend to continue studying the molecular mechanisms of drugs and drug-resistance in acute myeloid leukemia. To do this, I will be using multimodal data from single-cells, especially scRNA-seq and scATAC-seq data and developing tools to enable biological insights that can be gained from the integration of such datasets.

I will miss the Liau Lab dearly, however I am looking forward to potential collaborations with the lab in the future!
